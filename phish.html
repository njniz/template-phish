This experiment aims to evaluate your abilities to recognize cybercriminal conduct such as phishing email attacks. These attacks often seek sensitive data from their victims, such as logins and passwords, in order to gain access to the target's network or company.

There are many ways to avoid Phising:

Verify the Sender's Email Address

Always carefully check the sender's email address before responding 

or clicking on any links. Don't be deceived by email addresses that 

look similar but are actually different

Avoid Clicking Direct Links

Refrain from clicking direct links in emails. Instead, use official

websites or applications to access specific accounts or services.

Beware of Requests for Personal Information

No legitimate organization will ask for personal information, such as

passwords or credit card numbers, via email. If an email requests

sensitive information, do not provide it.

Report Phishing Emails

If you receive a phishing email, report it to your email service provider

and the relevant organization if possible. This helps reduce the risk

of scams for others.

Stay vigilant online. Think twice before clicking, and keep your personal information safe from phishing scams. 

Your awareness

 is your best defense against cyber threats.
